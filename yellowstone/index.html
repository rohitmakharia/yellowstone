Prep

Trip prep

Trip prep

A few things worth knowing before we fly out — how the two cars work together, bear safety for the kids, what to pack, and what the weather will probably do.

Two-vehicle ops

  • Suburban or Tahoe7 seats + luggage + AWD. Two of these handle 12 + bags.
  • Driver rotationNo one drives both Aug 26 transit AND Aug 27 Canyon. Whoever drives Aug 27 dawn gets the easy Aug 29 morning.
  • Comms between carsCell service is spotty in YNP. Set rendezvous landmarks each morning.
  • Bear spray4 cans total, 2 per vehicle. Buy in Jackson on Aug 22 — can't fly with it.
  • Parking pinch pointsGrand Prismatic / Fairy Falls fills by 9 AM. First vehicle parks; second drops then circles.
  • Cooler carOne vehicle is the daily cooler car. Stocked from rental kitchen the night before.

Bear safety briefing

  • Carry bear spray accessibleHolstered or in hip pocket — not buried in a backpack. Practice the safety release before you need it.
  • Make noise on trailsTalk, clap, sing. Surprise is what triggers attacks. Groups of 3+ rarely have incidents.
  • Never approach wildlife — everThe "tourons of Yellowstone" videos exist for a reason. Bison gore tourists every year. Stay 100 yards from bears/wolves, 25 yards from bison/elk/everything else.
  • If a bear bluff chargesStand your ground. Don't run. Spray within 30 ft, aim slightly downward — the spray rises.
  • If a grizzly attacksPlay dead. Face down, hands on neck, legs spread. Wait until the bear leaves the area.
  • If a black bear attacksFight back. Aim for the face and snout. Black bear attacks are rare and predatory; fight as hard as you can.
  • NPS official guidancenps.gov/yell/planyourvisit/bears.htm — required reading for everyone over 10.

Weather — what to expect

  • Daytime highs70–80°F at lower elevations, 60–70°F at 7,000+ ft. UV is intense even when cool — sunscreen and hat mandatory.
  • Overnight lows35–45°F. Mornings start cold — layers, even in mid-summer. A fleece + rain shell handles most days.
  • Afternoon thunderstormsCommon, brief, intense. Plan to be off exposed ridges and back near the car by 2 PM most days.
  • Late-August snow possibilityRare but possible above 9,000 ft (the Tram top is 10,450 ft). Bring a warm layer for the Tram day.
  • Sun + altitudeSunburns at altitude are faster and worse. Reapply sunscreen midday even on cool days. Lip balm with SPF.
  • Smoke from western wildfiresPossible in late August. Check air quality on AirNow.gov in the days before flying out.

Packing checklist

  • Layered clothingFleece, light puffer, rain shell, long pants, t-shirts, one warm hat. Mornings cold, days warm.
  • Hiking shoesSturdy walking shoes or hiking boots. Some terrain is rocky.
  • Sun gearHat with brim, polarized sunglasses, sunscreen SPF 30+, lip balm with SPF.
  • Water bottles1 per person, refillable. Hydrate aggressively the first two days at altitude.
  • Binoculars1 per family. Hayden Valley wildlife depends on them.
  • Day pack itselfCarry water, layers, snacks, sunscreen, phone, camera.
  • Phone power bankWildlife photography drains batteries. One per family.
  • Swimwear + flip-flopsFor the hot tubs at the rentals.
  • Headlamp / flashlightFor evenings outside or any pre-dawn wildlife runs.
  • Cash, small billsFor tips on the float trip, raft trip, restaurants.
  • Rakhi suppliesRakhis, sweets, and any traditional items for Aug 28 at the rental.
  • Dadu's birthday gifts & cardsFor Aug 23 at Glorietta. Coordinate so nobody duplicates.
  • Bear spray4 cans, 2 per vehicle. Buy on Aug 22 at any Jackson gear shop. Cannot fly with it.
